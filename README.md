# Virginia Nonprofit Economic Impact Analysis

## **Overview**
This repository contains the end-to-end data pipeline, NLP classification engine, and financial modeling used to power the **2026 Virginia Nonprofit Economic Impact Report**. [cite_start]Working with the **Center for Nonprofit Excellence (CNE)**, this project transformed millions of raw IRS records into actionable insights for Virginia lawmakers and community stakeholders[cite: 1, 197].

## **The Problem: The "Unclassified" Data Gap**
[cite_start]A significant number of nonprofits in the IRS Business Master File are unclassified, meaning they do not indicate a specific mission focus in their submission[cite: 9]. [cite_start]This lack of categorization makes it difficult to measure the specific economic footprint of vital sectors like healthcare or human services[cite: 80].

## **Technical Solutions & Project Goals**
Each file in this repository was designed to provide a specific, high-fidelity metric for the final report:

### **1. Sector Classification via NLP**
* **File:** `src/nlp_pipeline.py`
* [cite_start]**Goal:** Utilized **Latent Dirichlet Allocation (LDA)** to classify 43,115 charitable nonprofits operating in Virginia[cite: 7]. 
* [cite_start]**Impact:** Identified that while the smallest number of nonprofits focus on legal representation/crime reduction, the largest categorized sectors are **Education and Religion**[cite: 8].

### **2. Regional Revenue & Economic Modeling**
* **File:** `src/data_cleaning.py`
* [cite_start]**Goal:** Engineered an ETL pipeline to aggregate total revenue across various regions[cite: 81].
* [cite_start]**Impact:** Determined that the total revenue generated by nonprofits in 2023 was **$79,218,658,131**[cite: 79, 80].
* [cite_start]**Impact:** Identified that **Northern Virginia** generates **47%** of the state's nonprofit revenue[cite: 81].

### **3. Financial Distress & Vulnerability Analysis**
* **File:** `src/choropleth.py` & `notebooks/distress_analysis.ipynb`
* [cite_start]**Goal:** Calculated "Exposure to Grant Instability" by defining distressed nonprofits as those whose government grants exceed their remaining revenue after operating expenses[cite: 161, 163].
* [cite_start]**Impact:** Identified **173 distressed nonprofits** facing a combined budget shortfall of **$56.7 million**[cite: 174, 175].

### **4. Labor & Wage Metrics**
* **File:** `notebooks/employment_analysis.ipynb`
* [cite_start]**Goal:** Aggregated salaries and benefits from IRS 990 filings to illustrate the nonprofit sector's role as a major employer[cite: 63, 99].
* [cite_start]**Impact:** Quantified **$3,290 million** in direct compensation paid to Virginia employees in 2024[cite: 99].

## **Key Findings Summary**
* [cite_start]**Total Sector Revenue:** $79,218,658,131[cite: 80].
* [cite_start]**Leading Sectors:** Healthcare (41% of revenue) and Education (15%)[cite: 80].
* [cite_start]**Volunteer Value:** Quantified the impact of 2,177,183 volunteers, contributing labor worth an estimated **$5.2 billion**[cite: 138, 139].
* [cite_start]**Community Safety Net:** Documented that Virginia food banks provided **175 million pounds** of groceries in 2024[cite: 123].

## Data Limitations & Reconciliation
This project reconciles live directory data with historical tax filings. Users should note:
* **Temporal Mismatch:** The BMF is a "Live" snapshot (May 2026), while 990 financial extracts represent the 2023 tax year.
* **The "Lag" Effect:** Approximately 55% of organizations in the BMF lack granular financial data in the 2023 extracts. This is primarily due to the 12-18 month IRS processing window for digital returns.
* **Reconciliation Rate:** This pipeline captures **$23.14B (89.3%)** of the $25.91B benchmark cited in the CNE 2026 Economic Impact Report. The delta represents non-digitized 990-N filers and organizations currently in the IRS processing queue.
